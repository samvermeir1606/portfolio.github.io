Big boys in space

This 3rd-person co-op game was made together with 3 other teammembers for a schoolproject. The assignment to make a "vertical slice" of a game was met with a lot of positive feedback from fellow students, teachers and friends. My main focus during the development of the game was implementing the gameplay like e.g.: scrap that the player can pick up, generators that needs to be filled, doors that need to be opened, an enemy spawner, ability system and more.

Skills

The game

You and your buddy are crashed on an alien planet and need to find a way home. While blasting your way through the enemies, you can collect their scrap and fill the generators. These generators will grant yoou access to different parts of the map, including a beacon to call-in the dropship to come and pick you up. Defend your position while waiting for your crew to pick you up and you'll be on your way to the next level.
At the start of the game you can choose to play alone or with a friend and choose your preferred weapon and special ability. These abilities can range from better weapons, to automatic healing of your allies and more. Choose wisely because you'll encouter multiple different enemies with different tactics that want to eliminate you.

The team

This was my first group project where we had to finish a whole game (vertical slice), not just the prototype. The team consisted of Simon Verstraete (artist), Klaas Bulcaen (programmer), Aäron Alluyn (programmer), Bram Delaey (artist) and myself (programmer). It was my first time to fully finish a game and release it.

Conclusion

I had the most fun during the development of the project when showing our work to friends and family and getting their feedback and reactions on what we had created. This often gave a boost and inspiration to make or add new things to the game.
It was also during the making of this game that a realized that focussing on the actual gameplay of the game was one of my favorite things, more than other aspects of the development process.
